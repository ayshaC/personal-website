About Me

Hi! My name is Aysha, and I’m an 18 year old Engineering Student at the University of Waterloo. I’m interested in design and technology, and passionate about using these to create positive social impact. I think we can leverage innovation to make the world a better place. How am I doing this now? Working on fun and impactful projects, participating in hackathons, and joining groups that are making positive change on campus. If I’m not doing these things, you’ll probably find me running, exploring, or reading (fiction, tech blogs, some other stuff in between).


Projects

Fidget Spinners are small and addictive hand-held toys that spin with the flick of a finger. This past co-op term, I was part of a four-person startup that designed and manufactured fidget spinners. We sold over 200 spinners, generating a revenue of $3000. I worked with a designer to develop a single-page mobile responsive landing site. Built using HTML, CSS, and jQuery. The code can be viewed here.

Co-developed GoodTaste, a food recommendation web app prototype at StarterHacks 2017. The goal of our product was to help users find relevant ratings and reviews through a peer-to-peer network. Used HTML, CSS, JavaScript and Google API's. Winner of Mentor's Choice award for originality and creativity. Code can be viewed here.

For our design course, we were challenged with designing a mechanical device that helps older adults in their day-to-day lives. After implementing the User Design Process, to we came up with the Twist n' Lift, a car attachment that helps to relieve the stress of sitting and getting out of a vehicle. The four-month long process involved many iterations of brainstorming, generating use requirements, prototyping, and doing user-testing. Some of the tools we used were personas, quality function deployment charts, fault tree analysis and many more.
